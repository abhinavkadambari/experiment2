Apple Inc., founded in 1976 by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne, is a prominent American multinational technology company headquartered in Cupertino, California. Renowned for its consumer electronics, software, and services, Apple's product lineup includes the iconic iPhone, iPad, Macintosh computers, iPod, Apple Watch, and Apple TV. The company develops its own operating systems, such as macOS, iOS, watchOS, and tvOS. Alongside hardware, Apple offers a range of software and services like the iTunes Store, App Store, Apple Music, iCloud, and the iWork and iLife suites. With a global chain of retail stores known as Apple Stores, the company provides a platform for product sales, technical support, and educational activities. Apple is celebrated for its emphasis on design, user experience, and innovation, with a significant impact on the technology industry. Recognized for its financial success, Apple's valuable and profitable status stems from popular products, strong brand loyalty, and a cohesive ecosystem of hardware, software, and services.
